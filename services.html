Why it's important

  • Zero bloat - No unnecessary plugins, libraries, or mystery code

Why not just use something like WordPress or Wix?

Because those tools are built for speed, not quality. They load a lot of extra code, rely on plugins, and can be hard to customize properly. I build everything from scratch, so your site will be faster, cleaner, and built exactly for what you need—no more, no less.

How does this help your business?

  • More visitors stick around - If your site loads slow, half your traffic could leave

  • Higher search rankings - Google ranks sites that are fast and user-friendly
  • More trust and authority - Clean UX and stable performance build user confidence
  • Fewer tech headaches - No plugin updates, no theme conflicts, no surprise errors

Security That's Built In

Most WordPress sites are constantly under attack. Outdated plugins and unpatched themes are easy entry points for hackers. With a custom-coded static site, there's nothing to hack into. No login page, no admin panel, no database—just fast, simple HTML, CSS, and JS. It's the digital equivalent of locking all the doors and throwing away the keys.

High Google Page Speed Score

A high Google PageSpeed score means your website loads quickly, and that brings real benefits. People are more likely to stay on a site that loads fast—if it takes too long, they might leave before it even finishes loading. Fast websites keep visitors around longer, which increases the chances they'll reach out, buy something, or explore more of what you offer.

It also helps with SEO. Google uses page speed as a ranking factor, so if two websites are similar in content, the faster one usually ranks higher in search results. That means better visibility and more organic traffic. A high score also lowers bounce rates—people are less likely to leave out of frustration—and provides a smoother experience on mobile devices, which is where most traffic comes from these days.

And if you're running ads, fast sites tend to convert better and even cost less per click. Google rewards fast-loading pages with lower ad prices and better performance. So, high page speed is not just about bragging rights—it directly impacts how effective and successful your site can be.
